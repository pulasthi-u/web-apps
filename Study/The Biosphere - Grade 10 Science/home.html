Domain Archaea

Description
  • Single-celled organisms
  • Prokaryotic (having no cell nucleus, endoplasmic reticula, Golgi complexes, mitochondria, chloroplasts or lysosomes and no membrane-bound organelles)
  • Some have flagella
  • The oldest organisms on Earth
  • Found in extreme and harsh environmental conditions; e.g.,
    • Highly saline, acidic or basic environments
    • Volcanoes
    • Ocean beds
    • Deserts
    • Environments with a pressure of over 200 atmospheres
  • Reproduce through binary fission
  • Not sensitive to antibiotics
Examples
  • Halophiles (salt-lovers) e.g., Halobacterium salinarum
  • Methanogens (methane-producing) e.g., Methanosarcina
  • Thermococcus celer

Domain Bacteria

Description
  • Prokaryotic
  • Sensitive to antibiotics
  • Most abundantly found organisms; found almost everywhere
  • Morphologically of several types;
    • Cocci (e.g., coccus, diplococci, streptococci etc.)
    • Bacilli (e.g., coccobacillus, bacillum, diplobacilli, streptobacilli etc.)
    • Vibrio
    • Spirillum
  • Can be pathogenic
