Titanium

  • Titanium occurs naturally in rutile (\(\ce{TiO2}\)), and ilmenite (\(\ce{FeTiO3}\equiv\ce{FeO}\cdot\ce{TiO2})\)
  • Shows three main characteristics;
    • Excellent resistance to corrosion
    • High heat-transfer efficiency
    • High strength-weight ratio
  • Has a higher melting point than \(\ce{Al}\) and is therefore used over \(\ce{Al}\) in the aerospace industry where resistance to high temperatures as well as resistance to corrosion is necessary
  • Titanium is also used to replace hip joints
  • \(\ce{TiO2}\) is used as a white pigment in paints
  • \(\ce{Ti}\) shows the \(\ce{+2}\), \(\ce{+3}\), \(\ce{+4}\) oxidation states in \(\ce{Ti^2+}\), \(\ce{Ti^3+}\) and \(\ce{Ti^4+}\) respectively
  • \(\ce{Ti^2+}\) is a powerful reductant and may reduce water to hydrogen gas
