Manganese

  • Manganese occurs naturally in the mineral pyrolusite (as \(\ce{MnO2}\)), and rhodochrosite (as \(\ce{MnCO3}\))
  • Shows at least 5 stable oxidation states. The most commonly shown oxidation states are \(\ce{+2}\) and \(\ce{+7}\), and the more stable oxidation state is \(\ce{+2}\). Sometimes may even show oxidation states from \(\ce{-3}\) upto \(\ce{+7}\)
  • Elemental \(\ce{Mn}\) is somewhat reactive as it is electropositive
  • Reacts with oxygen to form the mixed-valent oxide \(\ce{Mn3O4}\) (similar to \(\ce{Fe3O4}\))
  • Does not react with water under normal conditions, but dissolves readily in acids
  • Manganese is mixed with iron to make an alloy called ferromanganese
  • \(\ce{Mn}\) is also a component of Vitamin \(\text{B-}1\)
