Cobalt

  • \(\ce{Co}\) is a somewhat less abundant metal. It is ductile and somewhat malleable
  • A naturally occuring magnetic metal
  • \(\ce{^59 Co}\) is the only naturally occuring isotope of \(\ce{Co}\)
  • \(\ce{^60 Co}\) is a radioactive isotope which emits gamma rays. It is used to treat cancer as its radiation kills cancer cells, and also used to test whether the body synthesizes vitamin \(\text{B-}12\) efficiently
  • Oxide ores of \(\ce{Co}\) are used in pottery and glassware to obtain a blue color. They are also used in the paints and ceramics industries
  • The most important use of cobalt is in superalloys (alloys which do not rust, and retain their physical and chemical properties even at high temperatures), which are used in airplane parts and gas turbines
  • Cobalt compounds are also used as catalysts
  • \(\ce{Co}\) does not react with water, but dissolves most in acids releasing \(\ce{H2}\)
  • \(\ce{Co}\) also combines with \(\ce{O2}\), but does not catch fire unless it is in the form of a powder
