Vanadium

  • \(\ce{V}\) has excellent resistance to corrosion at room temperature
  • Vanadium is mainly used to make steel alloys, which in turn are used to make high speed cutting tools, and springs
  • Adding a small quantity of \(\ce{V}\) to steel causes the formation of small grains of \(\ce{V4C3}\), which reinforce the steel and increase its strength
  • Vanadium pentoxide (\(\ce{V2O5}\)) is used as a catalyst in the Contact Process in the manufacture of sulfuric acid. \(\ce{V2O5}\) catalyzes the contact reaction as follows \(\ce{V2O5 + SO2 -> SO3 + V2O4}\) \(\ce{V2O4 + O2 -> V2O5}\)
  • Vanadium shows unusually many adjacent oxidation states; it shows the \(\ce{+2}\), \(\ce{+3}\), \(\ce{+4}\) and \(\ce{+5}\) oxidation states in various compounds
  • Ammonium metavanadate (\(\ce{NH4VO3}\)) is a common compound containing \(\ce{V}\) in the \(\ce{+5}\) oxidation state. It may be dissolved in sulfuric acid, or some strong acid to obtain a mixture of \(\ce{V(V)}\).
