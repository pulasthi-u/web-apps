Iron

  • \(\ce{Fe}\) is abundantly found on Earth, due to its extremely stable nucleus
  • Iron naturally occurs in hematite (\(\ce{Fe2O3}\)), magnetite (\(\ce{Fe3O4}\)), limonite, sidorite, iron pyrites etc.
  • Most commonly shows the \(\ce{+2}\) and \(\ce{+}\) oxidation states. Rarely shows the \(\ce{+6}\) oxidation state in ferrate (\(\ce{FeO4^2-}\))
  • Pure \(\ce{Fe}\) reacts readily with atmospheric oxygen and moisture and corrodes destructively (we call this rusting)
  • Shows magnetic properties
